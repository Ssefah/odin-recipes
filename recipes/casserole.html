Sweet Potato Casserole

Yummy Sweet Potato Casserole

A Thanksgiving staple, Sweet Potato Casserole is a delectable dish that delights with its creamy, sweet, and comforting flavors. This culinary masterpiece features a base of mashed sweet potatoes, infused with warm spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger, and enriched with butter, milk, and eggs. The mixture is then baked to perfection, resulting in a smooth and velvety texture that melts in the mouth.

Crowned with a delectable topping, Sweet Potato Casserole elevates the dining experience. A classic option is a buttery streusel of flour, brown sugar, and pecans, which adds a delightful crunch and a hint of caramel-like sweetness. For a more indulgent experience, marshmallows are often used, creating a pillowy topping that caramelizes under the oven's heat, adding a touch of sweetness and a marshmallow-y fluff to the casserole's richness. Whether adorned with a streusel or marshmallow topping, Sweet Potato Casserole is a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that will undoubtedly grace the Thanksgiving table and bring smiles to every diner's face.

Ingredients

    Sweet Potatoes:

  • 4 cups peeled, cubed sweet potatoes
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ½ cup milk
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt

    Pecan Topping:

  • ½ c½ cup chopped pecansup packed brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
  • ½ cup chopped pecans

Direction

  1. Step 1:

    Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).

  2. Step 2:

    Prepare sweet potatoes: Put sweet potatoes in a medium saucepan and cover with water. Cook over medium-high heat until tender, 10 to 15 minutes. Drain and transfer to a large bowl.

  3. Step 3:

    Mash drained sweet potatoes with a fork. Add eggs; mix until well combined. Add sugar, milk, butter, vanilla, and salt; mix until smooth. Transfer to a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  4. Step 4:

    Make topping: Mix brown sugar and flour together in a medium bowl. Cut in butter with a pastry cutter until mixture is coarse and looks like peas; don't overmix. Stir in pecans. Sprinkle topping over sweet potato mixture.

  5. Step 5:

    Bake in the preheated oven until topping is lightly browned, about 30 minutes.

  6. Step 6:

    Enjoy
